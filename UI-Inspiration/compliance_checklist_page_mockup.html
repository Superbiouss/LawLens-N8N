Failing requirements5 items
Liquidated damages must reflect genuine pre-estimate of loss
Indian Contract Act 1872, Section 74
Failing
Clause 3 sets $500,000 per breach "regardless of actual harm." Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act limits recovery to actual proven loss — a court will not award a punitive penalty. The clause as written is unenforceable in India, but you would still face the cost of defending any claim filed in Delaware.
§3 Penalties
Required fix
Remove "regardless of actual harm" and replace with "not exceeding actual documented losses arising from the breach." This aligns with both Indian and Delaware law.
Personal data processing obligations (DPDP Act 2023)
Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023, India
Failing
This NDA makes no mention of personal data handling, data fiduciary obligations, or consent requirements. If any confidential information shared includes personal data of Indian citizens, the DPDP Act 2023 applies and requires explicit data processing obligations in the agreement.
Required fix
Add a data protection addendum or clause specifying: nature of personal data shared, processing purpose, retention limits, and obligations of both parties as data fiduciaries under the DPDP Act.
Return or destruction of confidential materials on termination
Standard NDA requirement — Indian courts
Missing clause
No return-of-materials clause exists in this NDA. Indian courts expect this as a standard term in commercial confidentiality agreements. Without it, there is no clear obligation on either party regarding physical or digital copies of disclosed information once the relationship ends.
Required fix
Add: "Upon termination or request, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or certifiably destroy all Confidential Information and confirm destruction in writing within 30 days."
Reasonably defined confidentiality duration
Indian Contract Act 1872, reasonableness doctrine
Failing
The unlimited confidentiality term may be challenged under Indian courts' reasonableness doctrine. Perpetual restraints on trade or information are scrutinised carefully. While not automatically void, an unlimited term weakens enforceability and invites challenge.
Recommended fix
Replace unlimited term with a fixed 3-year term. This is defensible in both Indian and US courts and meets commercial expectations.
Governing law must be specified or mutually agreed
Code of Civil Procedure 1908, India
Failing
The agreement specifies Delaware law exclusively. If you are based in India and the breach occurs there, Indian courts may assert jurisdiction regardless. A one-sided foreign jurisdiction clause is often challenged in Indian courts — particularly by individuals, not corporations.
Required fix
Propose mutual jurisdiction in the defendant's home territory, or use ICC arbitration (New Delhi seat) as a neutral alternative acceptable to both parties.
Partial compliance2 items
Permitted disclosure carve-outs (legal compulsion)
Evidence Act 1872, India
Partial
Clause 7 includes a carve-out for legally required disclosures, but does not specify that the Receiving Party must give prompt notice to the Disclosing Party before complying. Indian practice and the Evidence Act expect this notice obligation to be explicit.
Recommended addition
Add: "…provided that the Receiving Party shall give the Disclosing Party prompt written notice where legally permissible prior to any such disclosure."
Stamp duty compliance (if executed in India)
Indian Stamp Act 1899
Check needed
If this agreement is signed in India or relates to property/business in India, it may require stamping under the Indian Stamp Act. Unstamped agreements are inadmissible as evidence in Indian courts. The requirement varies by state.
Action required
Consult a local solicitor to determine applicable stamp duty in your state before signing. Maharashtra and Delhi have different rates.
