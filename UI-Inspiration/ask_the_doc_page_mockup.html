Can I exit this NDA early? What happens after the business relationship ends?
2:14 pm
Based on this NDA, there is no early exit mechanism for you as the Receiving Party.

Clause 5 §5 Term states the agreement remains in effect indefinitely — and crucially, even after the business relationship between you and Acme Corp concludes, your confidentiality obligations continue without any end date.
Clause 1 — Confidentiality

This is a critical red flag. Standard NDAs expire after 2–5 years. With no sunset clause, you remain legally bound even if Acme Corp is later acquired by a competitor.

Give me the exact redline language I should propose for the confidentiality term.
2:16 pm
Here's proposed redline language you can use in your counter-draft:
Current (Clause 1) — remove
"…for an unlimited period of time."
Proposed replacement — insert
"…for a period of three (3) years from the date of disclosure of such Confidential Information, provided that obligations with respect to trade secrets shall continue for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law."
This is the most commonly accepted compromise language: a fixed term for general confidential information, with trade secrets carved out under their natural legal protection period. Most corporate counsel will accept this without significant pushback.

Is the $500,000 damages clause enforceable if I'm based in India?
2:19 pm
This is a jurisdiction-sensitive question. Here's the analysis:

Under the contract's chosen law (Delaware, USA): Liquidated damages clauses are generally enforceable if the amount was a reasonable pre-estimate of harm at signing — but the phrase §3 "regardless of actual harm" significantly weakens Acme's position. Delaware courts may refuse to enforce a penalty clause that is clearly punitive rather than compensatory.

If you're based in India: Indian courts apply Section 74 of the Indian Contract Act, which limits recovery to actual proven loss regardless of what the contract says. A court in India would likely not award $500,000 unless Acme could prove that exact loss — making the clause largely unenforceable against you in Indian courts.

However, if Acme chose to sue in Delaware (as per Clause 5), you would need to defend yourself there — which carries significant practical cost regardless of the legal outcome.
